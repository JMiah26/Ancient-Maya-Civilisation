## Ancient Maya Civilisation Environment

A 3D environment project based on the Ancient Maya Civilisation during the Classic Period.

This project explores Maya architecture, culture, and environmental design through research, sketching, experimentation, and 3D modelling. The aim was to create an environment for a game engine that reflects key parts of Maya life, including temples, palaces, ball courts, houses, altars, and natural features.

## Project Overview

The goal of this coursework was to build a 3D environment that shows how the Maya civilisation may have looked during the Classic Period, around 200 to 900 AD. The project focused on architecture, materials, colours, religion, and parts of daily life. Research was used to guide the visual design and modelling decisions for the final environment.

## Research Focus

The research looked at the Maya Empire during the Classic Period and explored where the civilisation was located, what kinds of buildings were common, and which materials were used. This included the Yucatán Peninsula, Guatemala, Belize, and parts of Honduras and El Salvador. The study also covered temples, palaces, stelae, altars, ball courts, and other important structures that shaped Maya architecture.

## Design Development

The development stage included early sketches, modelling tests, map planning, and colour experiments. Sketches were used to plan objects such as altars and temples. Modelling experiments in Maya helped test ways to build stepped structures, stairs, railings, and temple tops. This stage also helped identify problems with time, scale, and modelling workflow before the final build.

## Environment Layout

The map design included major Maya inspired structures and landscape features. These included temples, a palace, a village, a ball court, rivers, lakes, a bridge, altars, and pillars. The layout was planned to reflect the symmetry often seen in Maya architecture while keeping the environment clear and easy to read. Wildlife and rainforest features were also considered as part of the setting.

## Models Created

The project included a range of 3D models based on Maya structures and environmental features. These included:

- Village huts
- Larger houses
- Ball court
- Temples
- Palace
- Pillars
- Rivers and lakes
- Trees
- Altars and stelae

Each model was developed through basic polygon modelling techniques and then reviewed to see what could be improved.

## Tools Used

- Autodesk Maya
- Game engine for final presentation
- Basic shading and material tools in Maya

The modelling process used tools such as Extrude, Bevel, Merge, Insert Edge Loop, Lattice, Soft Selection, and UV mapping ideas for later improvement.

## What I Worked On

For this project, I worked on the full design process from research to development. This included historical research, concept planning, sketching, map layout design, 3D modelling, colour testing, and evaluation of the final assets.

## What I Learned

This project helped me improve my understanding of:

- Research based environment design
- Maya architecture and structure types
- 3D modelling workflows in Maya

- The importance of textures and UV mapping

## Challenges

One of the biggest challenges was scale. Some models were built larger than needed, which made it harder to keep proportions consistent and added extra work when resizing parts such as steps, entrances, and roofs. Another issue was the lack of detailed textures, which made some models look too simple or too clean.

## Future Improvements

If I continued this project, I would focus on improving the scale of the models, adding more detailed textures, using UV mapping more effectively, and refining the shapes to make the environment feel more realistic. I would also spend more time adding surface detail and small imperfections so the structures feel less uniform.

## Conclusion

This project gave me a strong base in research driven 3D environment design. It helped me explore how history, architecture, and modelling can work together in one project. While there are clear areas to improve, especially in texturing and scale, the final outcome shows a solid understanding of basic modelling techniques and environmental planning.
